General
Yes! TMS welcomes anyone interested in shadowing, helping others, or learning about medicine. The medical field involves many roles beyond doctors — from nursing to biomedical engineering to health policy. Whether you want to volunteer at a nursing home, publish in a medical journal, or learn wilderness medicine, there's a place for you at TMS.
TMS holds general assembly meetings throughout the year to announce events and opportunities like Junior Coordinator positions. There is no fixed weekly meeting schedule for all members — consistent participation happens through individual committees. Check the Calendar for upcoming dates.
TMS organizes six major annual events: Committee Carnival, Dinner With Doctors, Aid The Cause, Stethoscope Launch Party, Shear Kindness, and Spring Fling. Visit the Events page for full descriptions.
Committee applications launch once per semester. Students can also propose new committees between semesters. If you have an idea outside of the application window, email tmsexecs@gmail.com. The board is always open to new ideas that benefit the TMS community.
Yes! TMS has dedicated volunteering committees including Denton Nursing Home, Outreach, TAMS Alzheimer's Care, and Healing With Music. Educational committees like TMS Shadows, Biomedical Engineering, and Women in Medicine also provide hands-on engagement with the medical community. See the Committees page for the full list.
Yes! Juniors can apply as Junior Coordinators (JCODs) for Aid The Cause or the Stethoscope Journal, gaining hands-on experience in event planning and publishing. Juniors can also propose new committees during fall semester for spring implementation.
TMS members gain volunteering hours, shadowing opportunities, teaching experience, publishing credits, and fundraising skills — all while building community with peers who share an interest in medicine. These experiences strengthen college and medical school applications and create real impact in the Denton community.
